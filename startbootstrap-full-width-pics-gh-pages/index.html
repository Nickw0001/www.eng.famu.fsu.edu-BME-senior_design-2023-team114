Reverse Stemless Shoulder Implant

Team 114: Sponsored by Exactech


Our Project

Our project objective was to design a reverse stemless shoulder implant along with a testing apparatus to determine the most effective design.

Exactech, a company that develops joint implants, has provided Team 114 with the task of redesigning the current stemless implant for reverse use. They want to first improve the resistance of movement between the implant surface and nearby bone. Once this has been achieved, they want the design to be modified to make surgical revisions for the implant easier. Considering no metrics were available, a testing device was built to test different directional forces on the implant. These tests decided which redesign was most effective in reaching our goal. Once we picked the most effective design, further changes were made to make the implant easier for surgical revision.

Many factors cause wear on shoulder joints such as disease, age, overuse, and injury. If the joint wear is bad enough, it may result in shoulder replacement surgery. Luckily, the field of implants is evolving quickly in the modern era. With constant changes in this field, patients can now expect to relieve pain and reclaim lost ranges of motion. Two joint implants are available depending on the humerus bone quality. These implants include stemless and stemmed. If the humerus bone quality is good , the surgeon can use a stemless joint that is less invasive than the stemmed implant. If the humerus bone does not meet their standards, the surgeon will use a stemmed implant.

The shoulder implant variations apply to either a typical or reverse setup. The typical replacement uses an artificial joint setup resembling the anatomy of a human joint, while the reverse is considered to be the opposite of the natural placement of the ball joint. . For shoulder replacement surgery the type of implant depends on the condition of the rotator cuff. If the rotator cuff is intact and not torn, the surgeon will choose the typical shoulder replacement. If the rotator cuff is torn or damaged, the surgeon will choose the reverse shoulder replacement instead.

Deliverables

Key Goals: Create a device that can allow the patient to move their shoulder joint. The device may be built durable enough to exceed the usual device life of approximately 10 years. The device may contain cage feature(s). The device may include different fixation methods. The device should be optimized to withstand rTSA forces.

Primary Market: Surgeons, completely worn rotator cuff patients.

Secondary Market: Researchers who want to test the design technology for long term application.

Stakeholders: Tom Vanasse, Exactech, Dr. Arce, Dr. McConomy, Caterina Arnold, Saralynn Jenkins, Jordan Steverson are stakeholders for this project since they have an invested interest in the outcome. They are helping to guide the necessary steps needed to make this project successful in fulfilling its goal.

User Need: Will increase mobility in the shoulder joint. Helps users who have osteoarthritis, rotator cuff injuries, fractures, osteonecrosis, and inflammatory disorders relieve their pain. Users will be approximately 46-76 years of age. Device should be capable of replacement if issues arise. User should be capable of performing basic motions after recovery period.

Design Input: Create a device that can allow the patient to move their shoulder joint. The device may contain cage feature(s). The device may include different fixation methods. The device should be optimized to withstand rTSA forces.

Qualitative Target: The device may be built durable enough to exceed the usual device life of approximately 10 years. Minimum follow up of 24 months. Approximately 60% of patients should have little to no pain six months after stemless shoulder arthroplasty. Approximately 85% of patients should nearly no limitations to their daily lives two years post-surgery. Should work for 38-53mm.

Concept Generation: Crapshoot method, Forced analogy.

Concept Selection: House of Quality, Pugh Chart.
